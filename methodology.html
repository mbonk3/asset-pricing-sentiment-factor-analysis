Empirical Strategy

Methodology

A three-stage econometric framework designed to isolate the behavioral channel of investor sentiment in asset pricing.

Framework Overview

Three-stage research design

The central challenge is identifying the behavioral contribution of sentiment independently of macroeconomic conditions—which also drive both sentiment readings and factor returns. The analysis addresses this through sequential purification and regime testing.

  • 1

    Sentiment Orthogonalization

    Each of the seven sentiment indicators is regressed on six macroeconomic growth rates (industrial production, durable & non-durable consumption, services, employment, CPI). The regression residuals form the orthogonalized sentiment—capturing only the behavioral, non-macroeconomic component. All regressions use Newey-West HAC standard errors.

  • 2

    Factor Model Alpha Estimation

    1,147 long-short anomaly portfolios (decile 10 minus decile 01, value-weighted) are regressed against CAPM, Fama-French 3-Factor, and Fama-French 5-Factor models. Portfolios with significant alpha (p<0.05) across all three models are classified as "robust anomalies." Residuals from each regression serve as benchmark-adjusted returns.

  • 3

    Sentiment-Conditional Regime Analysis

    Each month is classified into high (top tercile) or low (bottom tercile) sentiment using the orthogonalized indicators. Mean benchmark-adjusted returns are computed within each regime. Statistical significance is assessed via Newey-West t-statistics (3 lags). Analysis is repeated across all 7 sentiment indicators, 4 models, and 6 geographic groups.

Data

Seven sentiment indicators

Covering both market-based and survey-based proxies, the indicator set spans retail investors, institutional managers, corporate employees, and derivatives markets, providing a multi-dimensional view of sentiment.

Baker-Wurgler (BW)

Composite index built from IPO volume, turnover, closed-end fund discount, dividend premium, and equity issuance. The canonical market-based sentiment measure (1965–).

Zhou Investor

Survey-based investor sentiment index from Zhou et al. capturing retail investor optimism. Highest correlation with Baker-Wurgler (r = 0.73 post-orthogonalization).

ICS — Michigan

University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment index, a broad measure of household confidence over the business cycle (1978–).

AAII Bull-Bear

Bull-Bear spread from the American Association of Individual Investors weekly survey—a direct measure of retail investor positioning and sentiment expectations.

VIX (inverse)

CBOE Volatility Index used as an inverse sentiment proxy. High VIX reflects fear; low VIX reflects complacency. Negatively correlated with Investor sentiment (r = −0.29).

Zhou Manager

Corporate manager sentiment from Zhou et al., capturing C-suite optimism about future business conditions and capital allocation intentions (2003–2017).

Zhou Employee

Employee-level sentiment index, reflecting workforce confidence in employment stability and firm prospects—a granular micro-level sentiment proxy.

Composite: Behavioral Sentiment Index (BSI)

A principal-component-weighted composite of the above indicators (where available) forms the BSI, designed to capture the dominant shared behavioral factor across all proxies. It achieves the highest High-minus-Low spread of all indicators (+0.98% monthly, CAPM, OAP Robust).

Asset Pricing Models

Benchmark: three generations of factor models

Robustness requires anomaly alphas to persist across all three model generations. Only portfolios significant in CAPM, FF3, and FF5 simultaneously are classified as robust anomalies and used for the main sentiment-conditional analysis.

CAPM

Rit − Rft = α + β·(Rmt − Rft) + ε

Single market factor. Abnormal return α = anomaly alpha. 71% of portfolios significant.

Fama-French 3-Factor

= α + βMKT·MKT + βSMB·SMB + βHML·HML + ε

Adds size (SMB) and value (HML) factors. 75% of portfolios significant—controlling for size and value actually reveals more anomalies.

Fama-French 5-Factor

= α + βMKT·MKT + βSMB·SMB + βHML·HML + βRMW·RMW + βCMA·CMA + ε

Adds profitability (RMW) and investment (CMA). Strictest model: 58% significant, 18% robust across all three.

Statistical Rigor

Addressing inference challenges in financial panels

Newey-West HAC

Monthly financial returns exhibit both autocorrelation and conditional heteroskedasticity. Newey-West standard errors with 4–6 lags are applied to all regressions, ensuring valid inference without parametric distributional assumptions.

Principal Component Analysis

PCA is applied to the orthogonalized sentiment indicators to assess dimensionality, identify common factors, and construct the Behavioral Sentiment Index. Analysis is run across four sample periods (Long, Modern, Manager, Full Overlap) to assess stability.

Variance Inflation Factor (VIF)

VIF diagnostics are applied to the orthogonalization regressions to detect multicollinearity among macroeconomic predictors. All factors pass the VIF<10 threshold.

FDR Correction (Benjamini-Hochberg)

With 7 sentiment indicators, up to 21 pairwise correlations are tested per sample period. Benjamini-Hochberg FDR control at 5% prevents false positives from multiple testing, ensuring only genuinely significant co-movements are reported.

Tercile Regime Classification

Each indicator's orthogonalized time series is divided into terciles: bottom 33% = low sentiment, top 33% = high sentiment. Middle tercile is excluded to sharpen the contrast. All classifications use full-sample tercile breaks to avoid look-ahead bias.

Breusch-Pagan Test

Applied as a diagnostic for heteroskedasticity in the orthogonalization regressions. Results guide lag selection for Newey-West corrections and validate the HAC approach.

Portfolio Data

1,147 anomaly portfolios from two leading libraries

Factor portfolios are drawn from two comprehensive, freely accessible sources covering decile sorts across dozens of anomaly characteristics.

📚

Open Asset Pricing (OAP)

Value-weighted decile portfolios for hundreds of cross-sectional anomalies. Coverage includes global markets across developed, emerging, and frontier economies. Long-short portfolios (decile 10 − decile 01) form the primary test assets.

📈

JKP Factor Library

Jensen, Kelly & Pedersen factor library providing alternative factor constructions and theme-level aggregates across the USA, global developed, and global markets. Enables cross-library robustness checks.
